## Meeting Transcription Workflow

Summary:
The group discussed recording meetings directly into repo-local
minutes and transcript artifacts. They explored always-on recording,
chunking long audio into smaller files, speech-activity detection,
storage costs, and whether low-cost hosted transcription is good
enough for routine use.

Decisions:
- Meeting recordings and transcripts should be treated as useful
  project artifacts.
- Hosted transcription is acceptable for routine use when cost is
  low and the workflow stays simple.

## Privacy And Recording Ethics

Summary:
The meeting covered the privacy implications of recording other
people, sending audio to LLM systems, and later checking edited
minutes into Git. The discussion weighed enterprise agreements and
local models against the social and ethical risks of broad
recording.

Decisions:
- Human review is necessary before sharing minutes or transcripts.
- Privacy concerns remain a gating factor for broader recording use.
