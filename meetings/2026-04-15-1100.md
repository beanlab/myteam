## Workflow Scope And Product Boundaries

Summary:
The meeting opened by questioning whether workflows belong inside
`myteam` at all. The group framed `myteam` as a progressive-
disclosure and content-distribution tool, while workflows look more
like a larger execution system that may need to work across multiple
agent backends and user interfaces.

Decisions:
- Continue treating `myteam` primarily as a content and
  progressive-disclosure tool.
- Explore workflows either as a plugin for `myteam` or as a separate
  project with light integration.
- Favor a more generic execution layer rather than coupling the
  design to one agent tool.

Tasks:
- [Everyone] Continue exploring a generic workflow engine with light
  `myteam` integration.

Open Items:
- Should workflows be a `myteam` plugin or a separate repository?
- What is the right abstraction boundary between execution,
  interface, and agent backend?

## Workflow Economics And Model Efficiency

Summary:
The group reviewed recent AI usage totals and connected them to the
cost of large context windows, vendor lock-in, and regressions in
hosted tools. They argued that deterministic workflows can lower
cost by resetting context between steps, eliminating unnecessary
control-flow reasoning, and making smaller or open models more
practical.

Decisions:
- Treat deterministic workflows as a path to better reliability and
  lower cost.
- Keep investigating workflow structures that reduce repeated
  reasoning overhead.

Tasks:
- [Unassigned] Continue evaluating how workflow structure can reduce
  token cost and improve determinism.

Open Items:
- How much practical value can smaller or open models deliver once
  workflows encode more of the process?
- How quickly will frontier-model economics force this shift?
